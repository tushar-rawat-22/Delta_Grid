# Missions 89–92

> **Explanatory summary**
>
> This page explains preserved DeltaGrid research and evidence for human
> readers. It does not replace the linked source records, change their results,
> reopen research, or authorize paper trading, live trading, capital deployment,
> ML, or autonomous execution.

## What this record covers

These records show the progression from the product reset and funding-carry
charter through three later negative research outcomes. They distinguish
protocol creation from evaluation and protected data from result-bearing data.

## Question investigated

The programme moved from open-ended platform building to finite falsification:
could funding and basis carry, conventional directional strategies, or a fixed
session-conditional spot exposure survive frozen development controls and
justify access to later stages?

## Evidence and controls

The product reset bounded the remaining research families, candidates, rescue
cycles, and promotion gates. Mission 85 then locked the funding-carry charter
before real-market collection. Mission 89 used a locked protocol to evaluate
the carry family; no development candidate existed, so the holdout stayed
untouched.

Mission 90 preregistered its directional tournament before development
evaluation. No directional candidate was eligible, and validation and holdout
were not opened. Mission 91 then recorded one untested, price-independent
session hypothesis and froze its windows and development-only rules. That was
a protocol decision, not a successful strategy. Mission 92 evaluated the
frozen session windows in development and rejected them before any validation
or holdout access.

## Result

The chronological result is:

1. Product reset: finite falsification replaced open-ended expansion.
2. Mission 85: funding-carry research charter locked.
3. Mission 89: funding and basis carry rejected and archived.
4. Mission 90: all directional tournament hypotheses rejected.
5. Mission 91: later session-conditional hypothesis recorded and frozen, not
   validated.
6. Mission 92: session-premium hypothesis rejected in development.

No selected candidate or validated alpha emerged, and later-stage access was
not justified.

## Why it matters

The sequence shows why preregistration is not evidence of success and why an
untouched later stage should remain protected after development failure. It
also preserves negative results across economically different families instead
of allowing a new label to erase earlier rejection evidence.

## What this does not authorize

Historical next-workstream language records what was contemplated then; it does
not authorize current research. These records do not reopen a rejected family
or authorize validation, holdout, paper, live, capital, ML, or autonomous work.
The [final freeze](../DELTAGRID_FINAL_FREEZE.md) controls current authorization.

Exact source records control precise values and historical wording. Any
discrepancy must be resolved in favor of the source record, not this summary.
